FLOW-XPR: BALANCING SPECTRAL FIDELITY AND PERCEPTUAL QUALITY IN NOISE-ROBUST BANDWIDTH EXPANSION VIA FLOW MATCHING

Abstract

Noise-robust bandwidth expansion must restore the missing high-frequency components of speech while reducing noise. Existing methods suffer from a persistent trade-off between spectral fidelity (reducing spectral distortion) and perceptual quality. To address this, we propose FLOW-XPR, a flow matching model conditioned on XEUS and PatchReplace, designed to achieve balanced improvements in both aspects. Our approach leverages conditional flow matching for high-frequency restoration. It is conditioned on noise-robust self-supervised features (XEUS), which provide semantic guidance to better distinguish speech from noise. We also newly introduce PatchReplace, a simple yet effective augmentation strategy. It replaces corrupted spectrogram regions with clean counterparts, reducing the information loss of conventional masking and the temporal inconsistency of mixing between samples. This encourages the model to learn more robust speech patterns. Experimental results show that FLOW-XPR outperforms baselines in terms of both spectral fidelity and perceptual quality.
